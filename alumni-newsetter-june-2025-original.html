Save the Date - Homecoming Weekend 2025 - Sept. 25-28
 
 
Dear Hayden,

In case you missed it: we enjoyed a wonderful long weekend celebrating Alumni Homecoming Weekend September 26-29.  In addition to the traditional events like the golf tournament, classic car show, parade, and Spirit of Philanthropy banquet, we enjoyed collecting and displaying memorabilia and artifacts from our 150-year history (Battle Creek College, Emmanuel Missionary College, and Andrews University).  We hosted all events in the Johnson Gym, which added a touch of nostalgia to our experience. 

The weather was beautiful, the atmosphere was relaxed and comfortable, and the conversations and stories inspirational!  A new feature this year was a student missions-focused Friday night vespers where we retraced our long and rich history of student missions (since 1966, when Andrews sent out the first student missionary for a school year), the multiple short-term mission projects current students are involved in, and a call to future mission service, both short term and career opportunities.
 
 
 
Founded in Faith, Forward in Mission:
Our 150-Year Journey
 
 
This mural was commissioned by the Office of Alumni Services in celebration of our 150th birthday. It traces our journey from Battle Creek College to current-day Andrews University, and was unveiled at the Spirit of Philanthropy banquet during Homecoming Weekend. It now has a permanent home in the Adventist Heritage Center in the James White Library.
 
 
 
About the Artist

Karen Jimenez Findley is a young author and illustrator from Mexico. Until recently, she was based in Michigan while her husband Christopher attended the SDA Seminary. Now living in Texas, she finds inspiration in nature, child-like curiosity and the feelings evoked by God's love. In her work, she uses primarily digital media but also loves experimenting with traditional techniques.

Karen has had the privilege of collaborating with clients on art and graphic design projects such as illustrating children's books, designing book and magazine covers, logos, t shirts, general illustration, and creating murals - both on outdoor projects and paintings.
Karen is currently studying at the National Autonomous University of Mexico to complete her Bachelor of Fine Arts degree.  To see and learn more about her work, find her on Instagram or visit www.FindleyArtStudio.com.
 
 
 
 
"Forward in Faith"—Historical Book Release
 
"Forward in Faith,” Meredith Jones Gray’s second university history volume, was unveiled and made available for purchase during Alumni Homecoming. This book is a follow-up to her 2002 publication, “As We Set Forth,” which documented the origins of SDA higher education at Battle Creek College, then on to the establishment of Emmanuel Missionary College. The latest volume continues our story from 1960 to 1990, during which time EMC became Andrews University.

Both "Forward in Faith” and “As We Set Forth” are available for purchase at the Andrews bookstore. Orders may also be placed on the Andrews University Press website or by emailing aubookstore@andrews.edu.

Lamson Hall Remodel
 
 
 
 
 
This summer, we were privileged to partner with Maranatha Volunteers International on a project to update Lamson Hall, the women’s residence hall, which was originally built in 1947 and 1967. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Approximately 60 people, including volunteers from multiple states and several countries, Plant Services and Residence Life employees, and contract workers, contributed to this project. Thanks to these dedicated individuals and our generous donors, 31 residence hall rooms and 12 suite bathrooms have been updated.

Altogether, with updates that have taken place over the past 12 months, 172 of our students now benefit from these changes this fall semester. We look forward to future collaboration with Maranatha in summers to come, through which we will continue to improve the campus living experience for our students.
